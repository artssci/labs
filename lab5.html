What is Editorial Design

Editorial design is the branch of graphic design that specializes in the layout and composition of publications such as books, magazines or newspapers. It includes the realization of the interior and exterior graphics, always from an aesthetic approach linked to the concept that defines each publication and in relation to the printing and reception conditions. In the artistic context, the dialogue between text and image is essential. When a publication is valued as an artistic object, the design broadens its meaning and identity. In this sense, editorial design is an artistic activity and the designer is an author since their work includes a series of aesthetic issues, as well as commercial ones. The main objective of professionals dedicated to editorial design is to achieve a harmonic unity between text, image, and layout that adequately conveys the message of the content, promotes the publication commercially and has an aesthetic value ... click to read the full article

Create a grayscale wireframe in Illustrator

A wireframe is a simple visual guide that represents the skeletal framework of a website or digital product. Think of it as the blueprint for your final design. You’re providing enough detail so that everyone knows the shape of the wall, but you’re not getting so deep into it that you’re giving exact details on the type of brick the walls should be made of (that comes later). Though wireframes are most often created by designers, they need to be basic enough so that everyone from other designers, stakeholders, devs, and users can understand the ideas.

A grayscale or low fidelity wireframe is the most basic type of wireframing. It’s so simplistic, that paper and pen will still suffice as a way to represent your ideas. Low fidelity wireframes are done in grayscale with a focus on layout and high-level interactions. UI elements and content are represented by basic shapes like squares, triangles, circles, and lines ... click to read the full article

In design, a grid is a system for organizing layout. The layouts could be for print (like a book, magazine, or poster), or for screen (like a webpage, app, or other user interfaces). Practice with the interactive tutorial below to learn how to:

  1. Create a new Web file in Illustrator
  2. Locate the Rectangular Grid Tool from Illustrator's toolbar

Continue using shapes to represent the content and media of your webpage

  1. Create two new Layers: One for the text boxes and another for the shapes representing media like photos and videos. Separating similar content in different layers will allow you to select and make changes to them more easily and quickly
  2. Within the first newly created layer, use the Rectangle tool to represent media such as text and photos
  3. Within the second newly created layer use the Type tool to communicate the type of content that goes in each section (e.g., logos and titles)
  4. Use different font sizes to communicate the content hierarchy (i.e., what should look larger, smaller, etc.)
  5. Continue using the grid layout to place and align the text and media.
  6. Once you have finished, hide the grid layout and export your wireframe

Create a high-fidelity wireframe in Illustrator

High fidelity wireframing takes what you put together in the low fidelity stage and sprinkles in more of the detailed elements. High fidelity wireframes include visual markers and branding signifiers like colors, graphics, and font style. UI elements look realistic and might even include textures and shadows. At this stage, a designer might also choose to add in images and copy. ... click to read the full article

Transform your grayscale wireframe into a high-fidelity wireframe

  1. Replace the text placeholders with the actual text that could go in the headings and paragraphs
  2. Choose a font family for each section of the text. Good practices of wireframing suggest limiting yourself to two fonts to make the hierarchy clear. At this stage, you are using typography to communicate the hierarchy of information.
  3. Use photos to represent media such as photos and videos. You can search for high-quality photos here: https://pexels.com/
  4. Select a color palette and try it out on your design. Try out the following webpage that simplifies the process of selecting a color palette as it allows you to extract the colors from an image and create a color palette from them: https://color.adobe.com/create/image
