About photo optimization for the web

Optimization for the web is the process of compressing images and setting display options for optimal use on the Internet. When you put images on the Internet, file size becomes important; you want to achieve a file size that is small enough to allow a reasonable download time, but large enough to preserve colors and details to your satisfaction. There are three major graphic file formats that are used on the web: GIF, JPEG, and PNG... click to read the full article

Optimizing your photo with Photoshop

Our goal is to reduce the weight of the photo to somewhere between 100kb and 200kb. This way, your photo will load faster on other people's devices. However, you also want your photo to keep a good quality and still look sharp. A good practice is for a webpageâ€™s total weight to be between 1 and 2 MB in size.

Check the following interactive tutorial to learn how to optimize/compress your photo for the web

22 STEPS

1. Go to File

2. Hover over Export and click Save for Web

3. Start by adjusting your image to the view.

4. Click Fit in View. This way you'll be able to see the full image in this window

5. Select any of the available file formats

6. JPEG is a common choice

7. Choose between a compression quality

8. You can try a medium compression

9. Or a low compression quality.

10. Or a low compression quality.

11. You'll see that the quality of the image changes, with each option

12. Let's stay with High for now

13. Let's stay with High for now

14. Select Progressive for the image to download/show incrementally when people access your website

15. You can choose to convert your image colors to sRBG. sRBG stands for Standard Red, Green, Blue. This is a color set suited for the web.

16. You can resize the image to compress it more

17. You can do this by providing a width and a height

18. Or by lowering the image percentage Try lowering it percentage to 80%

19. Every change you make will be reflected in the left-bottom corner. There you'll see that the size increases or decreases (e.g., 243K). You'll also see how long will your image take to download/display in a website (e.g., 45 sec @ 56.6Kbps)

20. Click Preview to view your changes

21. Click Save once you are finished

22. Save your image with a different name. Keep the original in case you want to do other transformations in the future

Transform your photo to Ascii Art

Let's continue experimenting with Ascii Art. Follow the next steps to transform your photo into Ascii Art.

  1. Go to the Ascii generator page: https://www.ascii-art-generator.org/
  2. Select Image to Monochrome Ascii Art
  3. Upload your photo
  4. Select a width below 100px and click Start
  5. Once it has been generated, click "Download result" and save it in the computer's Desktop
  6. Open the downloaded file and copy the generated Ascii Art
  7. Go to your HTML code in Glitch and add the tags <pre></pre> below the image tag
  8. Paste the Ascii Art between <pre></pre>. Remember that the <pre> tag allows the text format to remain unchanged. See the example below:
